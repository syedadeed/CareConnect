Central Government Health Scheme Government

Comprehensive healthcare for central government employees, pensioners and their dependents

About CGHS

The Central Government Health Scheme (CGHS) was started in 1954 with the objective of providing comprehensive healthcare facilities to Central Government employees, pensioners and their dependents residing in CGHS covered cities.

Currently, CGHS facilities are available in 75 cities through more than 350 Wellness Centers/polyclinics across India. The scheme provides both outpatient care and hospitalization facilities.

Key Features

  • Comprehensive medical care including OPD treatment and hospitalization
  • Access to both modern and traditional systems of medicine
  • Cashless treatment in empanelled private hospitals
  • Reimbursement of expenses for treatment in government hospitals
  • Online appointment system for OPD consultations
  • Teleconsultation services available
  • Doorstep delivery of medicines for chronic diseases
  • Health education and wellness programs

Eligibility Criteria

The following categories of persons are eligible for CGHS coverage:

  • All central government employees paid from central civil estimates
  • Central government pensioners receiving pension from central civil estimates
  • Members of Parliament
  • Ex-Members of Parliament
  • Judges of Supreme Court and High Courts
  • Freedom fighters
  • Employees and pensioners of certain autonomous bodies covered under CGHS
  • Accredited journalists
  • Dependent family members of all the above categories

Hospitalization

CGHS beneficiaries are entitled to cashless treatment in empanelled private hospitals for approved procedures. The scheme covers room charges, consultation fees, investigation charges, implants, and all other services provided by the hospital.

How to Apply

To apply for a CGHS card, eligible beneficiaries need to follow these steps:

  1. Download the Application Form

    Download and print the CGHS application form from the official CGHS website.

  2. Fill the Application Form

    Complete the application form with all required details. Ensure all information is accurate and complete.

  3. Attach Required Documents

    Attach necessary documents such as:

    • Proof of identity (Aadhaar Card/PAN Card/Voter ID)
    • Proof of residence
    • Recent passport-sized photographs
    • Pay slip or pension payment order
    • Proof of relationship for dependent family members
  4. Submit the Application

    Submit the completed application form along with all documents to your department or the nearest CGHS office.

  5. Collect CGHS Card

    Once approved, collect your CGHS card from the designated office. The card will contain details of all registered family members.

For more information and to download application forms, visit the official CGHS website or contact your nearest CGHS Wellness Centre.
